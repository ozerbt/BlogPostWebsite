BLOG POST 2 - The Idea That Failed...

fairly recently, I was one of the co-founders of a business called, Yummr. the idea of Yummr began when, me and my friend noticed while dining out - most restaurant menus were incomplete in terms of dietary/nutritional information alongside poor visual representations of dishes. We wanted to solve this problem by creating an application that provided a comprehensive and interactive menu for the customer using Augmented Reality. We went ahead and chugged along for 5 months (01/2022 - 06/2022) creating an MVP for this potential groundbreaking product. mid-April, we applied to cohort 18 at the 1819 Innovation Hub and got accepted into the program. This facility provided us with the necessary mentorship to further dive into our idea and probe if we could create a viable business out of it. We went through the 6 week program and came up with our problem statement, did customer discovery, created a business model canvas, researched the market etc. this all led into the final week in which - we would present our idea infront of potential investors and well seasoned entrepreneurs. Although the presentation went great, the idea itself was not really solving that big of a problem. We realized that restaurant menus being incomplete was honestly not the end of the world for a lot of people. Sure, people would love to see an image or AR model of the dish, but at the end of the day - who has the time to whip out their phone and scan a QR code; taking them away from the main reason they ate out in the first place, to for once, not be on that dammed little thing. Although the realization of this has caused us to shift perspectives, we are still optimistic about AR and plan to pursue it in different industries besides the restaurant space. I am still thankful for this amazing oppurtunity as 1819 has taught us how to take an idea, throw it into the ringer and see if itll actually become a business.

if you would like to check out the website for our idea, its still up and running on Yummr.io!

BLOG POST 4 - Facebook Ad assignment

I created a facebook advertisement poster for my business, Yummr. The general point of the advertisement was to draw new users onto the site and gauge how many of the impressions converted to actual users. unfortunatly, my advertisement has been "in review" for the past 3 days and im not exactly sure why. I set my audience demographic to be younger, tech savvy people that happen to be foodies. I currently do not have any data on link clicks, leads, reach etc. as the advertisement is still in review. I may try and create a new advertisement if the problem still persists by the end of the day. The poster itself is extremely eye catching as its a giant red stop sign with the words "this is an ad, visit Yummr.io". My assumption is that people are psychologically trained to stop at a stop sign or anything that is red, so they will stop and take the time to visit the link out of pure curiosity. Hopefully facebook will finally be done with the "in review" stage of things so I can test my hypothesis and see what happens with a $5-10 budget.
